Supported Devices

CorbinMesh can support any device supported by [Commotion], however we do have some tested nodes to make the choice easier.

The primary choice is whether you want an indoor or outdoor node. We are happy to have indoor nodes on the network, but you will have more success connecting to the mesh by setting up an outdoor node as they can reach and spread the network further than an indoor node can: wireless signals reach more people when they’re unimpeded by walls and ceilings.

If you’re really keen a hybrid solution is to have an outdoor node mounted with good access to CorbinMesh, and mesh with (or even run an Ethernet network cable to) an indoor node. This helps propagate the mesh into your home where you or your visitors might not otherwise be able to access it.

Tested

The following list of models have been tested and confirmed working by us. It is recommended that if you’re not confident with flashing, configuring or installing your own node you use one of these models as you will find support more readily available from community members as we have experience with them.

If you are not confident with flashing your own node and just want a ‘plug and play’ solution, we can flash and configure one for you. We will ask node hosts to reimburse the hardware costs, but are happy to flash, configure and test on our time. To make this easier for us, please choose one of the supported models below.

If you’re really stuck, you can contact us and we’ll try to help you.

Indoor

These have no protection from the weather and should be mounted indoors, perhaps near a window with line of sight to the mesh.
