Code of Conduct

It is expected from each and every user to abide by this code of conduct.

You are permitted to use CorbinMesh under the following conditions:

In a nutshell

Any activity that threatens the functioning, security and/or integrity of CorbinMesh or it’s users is unacceptable.

Responsibility

You are responsible for what you use the network for, what you put on the network and for securing your devices.

You are responsible for your actions and activity on this network.

Security

Since CorbinMesh is an open network, anyone can connect to it. You are responsible for securing your devices. Use the same caution you would on the wider internet.

It is suggested that you use firewalls and antivirus on devices connecting to CorbinMesh.

Explicit Content

Any content that is pornographic, explicit or otherwise unsuitable for persons under the age of 18 should be secured in such a way that it cannot be accessed without validating the users age. CorbinMesh will notify and fully cooperate with law enforcement if we becomes aware of any use of the Service in any connection with child pornography or the solicitation of sex with minors.

Civility

You support a civil environment and should not use CorbinMesh to threaten, intimidate, discriminate, harass, bully, abuse, defame, encourage bodily harm or the destruction of property. Nor should you encourage others to do the same.

Privacy

As CorbinMesh is an open network accessible to anyone and your data is traversing it, there is no guarantee of privacy. Use the same caution you would on the wider internet.

It is suggested that you use secure connections or encryption for your data.

Lawful Behavior

You do not violate copyright or other laws, including:

  • Any activity which threatens disrupt a service through denial of service (DoS) attacks, flooding or otherwise overloading a service.
  • Attempts to gain unauthorized or unlawful access to any network or service.
  • Attempts to circumvent any security measures or user authentication of any host, network or user account.
  • Forging of headers within any network protocol.
  • Any activity which threatens the network by knowingly distributing software that contains a virus or any other malware components.
  • Sending mass messages where the content is not relevant or appropriate for the majority of recipients (i.e. Spam)

No Harm

You do not deliberately harm our infrastructure.
